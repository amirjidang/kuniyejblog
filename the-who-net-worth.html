The Who’s Discography

The Who has an extensive discography, with numerous albums released throughout their career. Their albums not only showcase the band’s musical evolution but also feature some of their most iconic songs. Here are a few highlights from The Who’s discography:

“Tommy”

Released in 1969, “Tommy” is a rock opera that tells the story of a deaf, dumb, and blind boy who becomes a pinball champion. This concept album features hits like “Pinball Wizard” and “See Me, Feel Me” and is considered a groundbreaking work.

“Who’s Next”

Released in 1971, “Who’s Next” is regarded as one of The Who’s greatest albums. It includes popular tracks such as “Baba O’Riley,” “Behind Blue Eyes,” and “Won’t Get Fooled Again.” The album showcases the band’s signature sound and captures their energy and rebellious spirit.

“Quadrophenia”

“Quadrophenia,” released in 1973, is a concept album that delves into themes of teenage angst, rebellion, and identity. The album features notable tracks like “Love, Reign o’er Me,” “The Real Me,” and “5:15.” It is considered a masterpiece and has become a fan favorite.

These albums are just a glimpse into The Who’s extensive discography. Their body of work spans across multiple decades and showcases their musical prowess and ability to captivate audiences with their unique sound.
