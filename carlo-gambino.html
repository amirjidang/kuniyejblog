Carlo Gambino Professional Life and Career

Carlo became involved in minor crimes like gambling, theft, and robbery as a member of the “Young Turks.” During “Prohibition” in the early 1920s, he also got involved in bootlegging. Carlo had become a dreaded mobster by the middle of the 1920s. Compared to the traditional mafia rulers, the “Young Turks” had a very different vision of how organized crime would develop. While the ancient mafia bosses were too proud of their Italian background, they believed that they would need to form alliances with more mafia factions if they wanted to do business in peace. Carlo prospered in the new mafia system because it was better organized and less violent. He continued engaging in illegal activities like gambling and extortion while developing schemes to make quick cash. He earned significant money illegally by selling Second World War ration stamps on the black market. Gambino was an employee of Albert Anastasia and had a very low-key criminal lifestyle. However, by the late 1950s, his boss had developed a severe eccentricity. He had turned violent to become extremely dangerous to the illegal house.

In 1957, Carlo emerged as the new Godfather of the family because there was no other candidate. Under his direction, the Gambino family increased its dominance nationwide and amassed enormous wealth. Carlo was earning hundreds of millions of dollars annually, further solidifying his position and elevating him to the status of one of the most potent crime lords in the entire nation.
