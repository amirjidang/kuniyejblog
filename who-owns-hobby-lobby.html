Who Owns Hobby Lobby? Hobby Lobby Company History

Hobby Lobby, a renowned arts-and-crafts retailer, has captured the hearts of creative enthusiasts since its founding in 1970. The company’s captivating journey began with David and Barbara Green’s entrepreneurial spirit and a mere $600 loan, as they embarked on a mission to create something extraordinary. Today, Hobby Lobby stands tall as the largest privately owned arts-and-crafts retailer globally, with a strong presence spanning across 48 states.

From its humble beginnings producing miniature picture frames, Hobby Lobby has evolved into a beloved brand synonymous with creativity and craftsmanship. With over 1,000 stores and a diverse range of arts and crafts products, Hobby Lobby continues to inspire countless individuals to unleash their creative potential. But who exactly owns this arts-and-crafts empire? Let’s delve into the fascinating history of Hobby Lobby and uncover the driving forces behind its success.

Key Takeaways:

  • Hobby Lobby was founded in 1970 by David and Barbara Green with a $600 loan.
  • It has grown to become the largest privately owned arts-and-crafts retailer globally.
  • Hobby Lobby operates over 1,000 stores across 48 states.
  • The company offers a wide range of arts and crafts products, catering to creative enthusiasts.
  • David Green and his family are the proud owners and driving forces behind Hobby Lobby’s success.

Hobby Lobby’s Corporate Headquarters and Operations

Hobby Lobby’s corporate headquarters are located in Oklahoma City, Oklahoma. The headquarters span over 12 million square feet, encompassing manufacturing, distribution, and an impressive office complex. This centralized hub serves as the operational base for the company’s extensive retail network.

The company operates multiple distribution centers strategically placed across the country to support the smooth flow of products to its stores. These distribution centers play a crucial role in meeting the demands of Hobby Lobby’s loyal customers.

In addition to its domestic operations, Hobby Lobby has established offices abroad to facilitate sourcing and international operations. The company has offices in Hong Kong, Shenzhen, and Yiwu, China, which enable the procurement of unique and high-quality products from around the world.

With its expansive corporate headquarters, distribution centers, and international offices, Hobby Lobby has built a robust infrastructure that enables efficient operations and seamless supply chain management.

    Hobby Lobby’s Retail Strategy

    Hobby Lobby follows a unique retail strategy that sets it apart from other arts-and-crafts retailers in the market. With a focus on store locations, big-box facilities, and retail space savings, the company has successfully built a strong presence across the United States.

    Store Locations

    Hobby Lobby strategically chooses store locations in mid- to high-income suburban areas, ensuring accessibility and convenience for its target customers. The company aims to serve communities that have a strong demand for arts and crafts supplies, allowing customers to easily find and shop at their preferred stores.

    Big-Box Facilities

    To optimize its retail operations, Hobby Lobby rents big-box facilities, such as previously occupied supermarkets or hardware stores. These spacious locations provide ample room for the company’s wide range of products, allowing customers to browse through an extensive selection. Moving into existing facilities also helps Hobby Lobby save on lease costs that would be associated with constructing new retail spaces.

    Retail Space Savings

    By utilizing big-box facilities, Hobby Lobby achieves significant retail space savings compared to traditional modular stores. The company’s stores can range in size up to 90,000 square feet, offering plenty of room for browsing and showcasing its diverse product range. These larger store formats also contribute to a sense of variety and abundance, attracting customers from a 10-15 mile radius.

    Hobby Lobby’s Controversies

    Hobby Lobby, a prominent arts-and-crafts retailer, has been embroiled in several controversies that have sparked debates about the intersection of religion, corporate social responsibility, and ethical practices.

    1. Burwell v. Hobby Lobby

    In a landmark Supreme Court case, Burwell v. Hobby Lobby, the company’s religious beliefs were cited as grounds for objecting to providing access to certain forms of contraception to its employees. This case ignited a nationwide conversation about the delicate balance between religious freedom and women’s reproductive rights.

    2. Allegations of Discrimination

    Hobby Lobby has also faced accusations of discrimination, with some employees claiming that they have experienced bias based on their gender, race, or religious beliefs. These allegations have raised concerns about the company’s commitment to fair treatment and equal opportunities for all its workers.

    3. Artifacts Smuggling

    In 2017, Hobby Lobby was fined $3 million for smuggling ancient artifacts from Iraq. The company had acquired a large number of cuneiform tablets and clay bullae, which were later forfeited and returned to their country of origin. This incident raised questions about Hobby Lobby’s adherence to ethical sourcing practices and respect for cultural heritage.

    4. Employee Safety during the COVID-19 Pandemic

    During the COVID-19 pandemic, Hobby Lobby faced criticism for allegedly endangering its employees by keeping stores open despite safety concerns. Employees reported inadequate safety measures, lack of social distancing, and insufficient communication from management. These allegations underscored the importance of prioritizing employee welfare and ensuring a safe working environment during unprecedented times.

    5. Corporate Social Responsibility

    The controversies surrounding Hobby Lobby have also highlighted the concept of corporate social responsibility (CSR). Critics argue that the company’s religious beliefs should not exempt it from upholding ethical standards and being accountable to its stakeholders, including employees, customers, and the wider community.

    While Hobby Lobby remains a popular destination for arts and crafts enthusiasts, the controversies it has faced serve as reminders of the need for transparent corporate practices, responsible decision-making, and a commitment to fostering an inclusive and socially responsible business environment.

    Hobby Lobby’s Ownership Transition

    In a recent announcement, Hobby Lobby’s founder, David Green, revealed that he has transferred ownership of the company’s voting stock to a trust. This strategic ownership transition ensures that the future stewardship of Hobby Lobby can pass from one generation to another while upholding its cherished Christian values and commitment to philanthropy.

    The decision to transfer ownership reflects David Green’s desire to leave a lasting legacy and utilize the company’s resources for meaningful and impactful philanthropic endeavors. As a family-owned and faith-centered organization, Hobby Lobby has a long-standing tradition of giving back to the community.

    Fun Fact: Did you know that Hobby Lobby’s philanthropic efforts go beyond the typical corporate giving? In fact, the Green family, who own and operate Hobby Lobby, donate a remarkable 50% of their annual profits to support various charitable causes.

    Through this ownership transition, Hobby Lobby seeks to ensure a seamless continuation of its values-driven mission. By placing the company’s voting stock within a trust, the Green family demonstrates their commitment to sustain the company’s philanthropy and community engagement for generations to come.
