How Khloe Kardashian's Net Worth Reached $60 Million

Khloe Kardashian is a reality star, socialite, businessperson, and one of the most recognizable people on the planet. She started out as a TV star but has managed to grow her wealth from just a few thousand to millions. Although she’s not as rich as her sisters Kim Kardashian and Kylie Jenner, she has millions in the bank, much more than most. Khloe got her big break when her family was cast in their own reality television series, Keeping Up With The Kardashians, on E!

Khloe utilized the platform from the show and forged an impressive business empire. It's no wonder she is estimated to earn $15 million annually from her businesses, brand deals, and television appearances. Here’s a closer look at how Khloe Kardashian grew her net worth over the years to $60 million.

10 Khloe Kardashian's Early Work Life

Khloe Kardashian was born in California on June 27, 1984, to Kris Jenner and the late Robert Kardashian. Khloe is the youngest of her three sisters, Kim and Kourtney Kardashian, and the oldest sister to Rob Kardashian, Kylie Jenner, and Kendall Jenner. After completing high school, Khloe got her first job as a personal assistant to her former schoolmate, Nicole Richie.

No one knows how much she was paid for the role, but it must have been a good side hustle that her sister Kim got a similar job for other celebrities like Paris Hilton.

9 Khloe Kardashian Earned Steady Income With Keeping Up With The Kardashians

Keeping Up With the Kardashians began in 2007 on E! and heavily featured Kim, Khloe, and Kourtney as they navigated their young adult lives. After 20 successful and entertaining seasons and 11 spin-offs on the network, the family had become big names in the entertainment industry.

Khloe was reportedly paid between $4.5 - $5 million per season from 2007 to the show's end. On top of her salary, she made more through the social media platform Keeping Up With the Kardashians provided her.

8 Khloe Kardashian Got A Huge Paycheck When Keeping Up With The Kardashians Ended

In 2015, Kris Jenner negotiated a multi-year deal with E! worth $100 million to see them release more seasons from 2017 to 2020. The show stars, Khloe, Kim, Kris, Kourtney, Kylie, and Kendall split the earnings from the deal equally, with the Good American co-founder walking away with at least $16 million.

7 Khloe Kardashian Is An Accomplished Author

While at the height of their reality television fame, Khloe Kardashian and her sisters ventured into the book business. They launched their first book Kardashian Konfidential, in 2010. After years of struggling openly with her weight, in 2015, Khloe released her memoir about weight loss, the bestseller Strong Looks Better Naked.

6 Khloe Kardashian Has A Career In Television

While with E!, Khloe Kardashian also made a good salary from spin-offs such as Kourtney and Khloe Take Miami, Dash Dolls, Khloe & Lamar with her ex-husband, Lamar Odom, and the special she starred and produced, 2017’s Revenge Body with Khloe Kardashian. Khloe also earned money through other opportunities away from her famous family, like her TV hosting gigs and show appearances.

The Celebrity Apprentice, Law & Order: LA, and 90210 were some shows she was paid to appear in during this time. Khloe also hosted a few Grammy and Oscar Award specials on E! and co-hosted 2012’s X Factor USA alongside actor Mario Lopez and her own talk show Kocktails with Khloe Pop Culture on FYI. Most recently, in 2021, Khloe hosted the first ever Candy Crush Suga tournament.

5 Khloe Kardashian Earns A Lot For Posts On Instagram

Khloe Kardashian has 311 million followers on Instagram, much more than stars like Justin Bieber and Taylor Swift. Because of her large following, Khloe charges a hefty sum to brands if they want to advertise with her. According to Vogue, The Kardashian star makes up to $1,320,000 for one brand post on her Instagram.

A lot of Khloe’s income comes from her work as an influencer. She has done campaigns for food, jewelry, lifestyle products, and footwear brands like Burst Oral Care, PJ Palace, KKW, and Kylie Cosmetics. In addition, Khloe also has several endorsements that pay her large amounts of money to represent them. She got endorsement deals with brands like Calvin Klein.

4 Khloe Kardashian Makes Huge Real Estate Profits

Like many celebrities who can afford to buy million-dollar mansions, Khloe Kardashian has a good amount of her wealth tied up in her property. After separating from Lamar Odom in 2014, she purchased Justin Bieber’s mansion for $7.2 million and redesigned it with the help of Martyn Lawrence Bullard to fit her modern style.

In 2020, she sold the home for $15 million, making double in profits compared to what she purchased it for. In October of the same year, she purchased a $17 million home in Hidden Hills and rebuilt it from scratch before moving in November 2021. Kris Jenner also moved in next to her daughter after purchasing a $20 million plot for her mansion.

3 Khloe Kardashian's Car Collection Adds To Her Net Worth

You’ve probably seen Khloe Kardashian pose in front of one of her cars on her Instagram account. Like her sisters, Khloe’s garage has luxurious rides, including a Porsche, Mercedes-Benz G6, four Rolls-Royce, a Ferrari, and a Lamborghini. Her impressive car collection is estimated to be worth $3 million, adding to her $60 million net worth.

2 Khloe Kardashian Makes Millions With The Kardashians Show On Hulu

After Keeping Up With The Kardashians ended, Khloe and her family sought better deals and signed with Hulu to create a new reality show. Stylecaster estimates Khloe makes between $7.5 million to $8.3 million per season for their mega-hit series, The Kardashians.

Khloe and her sisters not only star in the show, but they also serve as executive producers. The Kardashians are on its third season, and it looks like it will keep going for a while, meaning the reality star earns a steady income yearly.

1 Khloe Kardashian’s Good American Brand Earns Her A Huge Income

In 2016, Khloe Kardashian launched the jeans clothing line alongside Emma Grede, and on day one, it made $1 million in sales. The same year, she began her relationship with the father of her children, Tristan Thompson. The company has earned her a lot, as she was worth $20 million in 2015 and is now worth $60 million.

Good American is now worth $1 billion and is expected to exceed $200 million in revenue this year alone, proving how profitable it is to Khloe’s business portfolio. The mother of two’s net worth significantly increased after co-founding the jeans brand catering that caters to women from size 0 to 24. Good American has since increased its collection to dresses, swimwear, jumpsuits, and workout clothes.

Khloe Kardashian’s earnings from her reality show, clothing line and brand deals, and future business opportunities will no doubt keep her net worth rising for several years.
