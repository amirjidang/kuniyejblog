The hit television series, Mad Men, is one of the most critically and commercially successful television shows of all time. With an IMDb rating of 8.6 and a 94% score on Rotten Tomatoes, it is clear that people love the show.

While it came to its end in 2015, the show still lives on through the streaming service, Netflix, where all 92 episodes throughout its seven seasons can be relived in their entirety, much to the happiness of the show's hardcore fanbase.

Even though there are some characters have come and gone, the majority of the main cast are part of the show from the start to finish, and within this article, we will rank them in terms of net worth.

Kiernan Shipka (Net Worth: $2 Million)

As an incredibly young actress, to already have a net worth of $2 million is a huge deal for Kiernan Shipka, as it will only going to continue to grow as her career progresses.

Within Mad Men, Shipka plays Sally Draper who slowly gains meatier storylines, mainly due to Shipka's fantastic performance.

Since then, her career has only continued to grow as she is now the lead on Netflix's Chilling Adventures of Sabrina, showing just how quickly her star is on the rise.

Aaron Staton (Net Worth: $4 Million)

As Ken Cosgrove, Aaron Staton is part of the Mad Men series all the way throughout, with Ken working in the advertising world and rising in the ranks, while also working his writing side hustle.

While Ken Cosgrove isn't a main character on the show, he is often used to provide comedic relief when things become too serious.

Outside of Mad Men, Staton has been in Descent and Without A Trace, as well as providing voiceover work for video game L.A. Noire, helping him earn a net worth of $4 million.

Rich Sommer (Net Worth: $4 Million)

Rich Sommer plays a very fun character in the Mad Men world, as Harry Crane, who may not always be the central focus, but is a constant within the show from the very start.

Crane works as the head of the television department and constantly has to prove his worth, no matter how many good things he does for the company.

Sommer has been part of some huge television franchises throughout his career, having roles in Ugly Betty, GLOW and The Office, so it is no surprise he has a net worth of $4 million.

Vincent Kartheiser (Net Worth: $5 Million)

Vincent Kartheiser portrays the controversial Pete Campbell, someone who can be quite slimy at times, frustrating many of the characters on the show and the audience at home.

However, Kartheiser plays the role to perfection, as Campbell still remains likable at times due to his ability to pipe in some comedic moments that show a totally different side to his character.

Aside from Mad Men, Kartheiser has made his $5 million net worth through roles in Money, Angel and The Hairy Bird, although he will always be most known for his work in the world of 1960s advertising.

Jessica Paré (Net Worth: $6 Million)

As Megan Draper, Jessica Paré may not have been in Mad Men from the very star, but once she is part of it, she certainly makes her mark, as Don's second wife...although that marriage doesn't last either.

Megan's attempts to break into acting certainly have mixed success, but she is a character who fans really enjoyed, with Jessica Paré doing a fantastic job.

Throughout her career in acting (in real life), Paré has managed to earn a net worth of $6 million, and while Mad Men is arguably her biggest role to date, she has also appeared in Life and Stardom, as well as other works.

John Slattery (Net Worth: $10 Million)

John Slattery is a vastly experienced actor who has had an incredibly successful career, which has seen him grow a net worth of $10 million, and although he has been in many different roles, it is Mad Men that he is best known for.

That isn't a bad thing either, as Slattery plays one of the show's main characters, Roger Sterling, who is in charge of the advertising company and remains in a position of power throughout the show.

Away from Mad Men, many people recognize Slattery from the Marvel Cinematic Universe, where he portrays Howard Stark, father of Tony, appearing in several movies including Avengers: Endgame.

January Jones (Net Worth: $10 Million)

January Jones portrays the often conflicted Betty Draper, with Betty being a character who is forced to go through a wide range of different emotions on the show, which Jones performs incredibly.

Jones was fantastic within this series, playing a key role throughout, even if she doesn't appear quite as much once she is separated from her husband, Don Draper.

However, away from Mad Men, Jones has worked in several blockbuster movies such as Unknown and X-Men First Class, helping her develop a net worth of $10 million.

Christina Hendricks (Net Worth: $10 Million)

Mad Men is a show that is jam-packed with incredibly strong and powerful women, and Christina Hendricks portrays one of those confident characters.

Hendricks plays Joan Holloway on the show, who earns her way into a position of power within Sterling Cooper by her own hard work and smart thinking, constantly being one step ahead of everyone else.

Throughout her acting career, Hendricks has appeared in countless television shows such as ER, Firefly, Presidio Med and Tru Calling, building a net worth of $10 million.

Elisabeth Moss (Net Worth: $14 Million)

As we mentioned, even though Mad Men takes place in the male-dominated world of advertising, it is actually the women who run the show, and Elisabeth Moss' Peggy Olson is the absolute best example of that.

Peggy rises to be in the same position as the men on the show, moving from simply being Don Draper's secretary to being an equal whom the powerful man cannot live without, as much as he doesn't want to admit it.

Since Mad Men, Moss has continued her great success, starring in Handmaid's Tale. She's also had plum roles in popular movies such as Get Him To The Greek and Girl, Interrupted, growing a net worth of $14 million.

Jon Hamm (Net Worth: $30 Million)

Jon Hamm is at the heart and center of everything that happens in Mad Men, portraying the main character of the show, Don Draper.

Hamm showcased his incredible personality, managing to make people love Draper as a character, despite the fact that he would often do things that would normally be hated on any other television show.

It was through Mad Men that Hamm really got his big break, but since then he hasn't looked back, growing a net worth of $30 million by starring in movies such as Baby Driver and Tag.
