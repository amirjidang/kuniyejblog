The death of Inger Stevens, a Swedish-American film, television, and stage actress, on April 30, 1970, remains controversial even today, and fans are curious to know more about the actress death.

Stevens was born in Stockholm, Sweden, on October 18, 1934. She grew up in Manhattan, Kansas, and New York City, New York, and completed her education at the Actors Studio and Manhattan High School.

The Farmer’s Daughter, a television series that aired in the 1960s, featured Inger as Katy Holstrum. Inger’s breakthrough performance paved the way for other high-profile parts on both big and small screens.

She received a Golden Globe nomination and an Emmy nod for this quick-witted, change-of-pace comic character, which ran for three seasons.

Why Did Inger Stevens Kill Herself? Suicide Or Murder

Inger Stevens, a blonde actress who portrayed the attractive housekeeper for three years on “The Farmer’s Daughter,” was discovered dead in her home on April 30, 1970. Acute barbiturate intoxication was cited as the actress’s cause of death which was ruled a suicide.

Lola McNally, Stevens’ roommate, and companion discovered Stevens dead on the kitchen floor of her Hollywood Hills residence. According to McNally, Stevens tried to talk but was unable to produce a sound as she opened her eyes and raised her head.

McNally informed the police that she had spoken to Stevens the night before and had not observed any signs of unrest. On the route to the hospital, Stevens passed away in the ambulance.

When medical personnel arrived, they took off a little bandage from her chin, revealing a small quantity of new blood flowing from a cut that looked to have occurred a few hours earlier.

Stevens was later referred to by Windom as “a woman with many secrets.” The full impact of that statement’s veracity wouldn’t be felt until after her passing.

Inger Stevens Husband And Parent’s Details
