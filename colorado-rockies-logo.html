    The Colorado Rockies Team History

    The Colorado Rockies were established as an NHL expansion team in 1974 under the name Kansas City Scouts. After two seasons in Kansas City, the franchise made its way to Denver in 1976 and became known as the Colorado Rockies. Despite their presence in the NHL, the team faced numerous challenges and struggled to achieve significant success throughout their time in Denver.

    After six seasons in Colorado, the team made the decision to relocate once again, this time to New Jersey. In 1982, the franchise officially became the New Jersey Devils, bidding farewell to their name and identity as the Colorado Rockies. The move brought about a fresh start for the team and allowed them to rebuild and eventually find success in New Jersey.

    While the Colorado Rockies may not have had a long-lasting impact on the ice, their place in NHL team history remains significant.
